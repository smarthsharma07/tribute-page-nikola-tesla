A Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. He is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Tesla held over 300 patents worldwide and worked on innovations that shaped modern technology, including wireless communication, radio, X-ray imaging, and electric motors.

  • 1856 — Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • 1884 — Moved to the United States and worked briefly with Thomas Edison
  • 1888 — Developed the AC induction motor
  • 1891 — Invented the Tesla Coil
  • 1943 — Died in New York City

Despite his brilliance, Tesla struggled financially later in life. Today, he is recognized as one of the greatest inventors in history, whose ideas laid the foundation for wireless energy and modern power systems.
